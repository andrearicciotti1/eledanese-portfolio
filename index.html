About

Eleonora Danese is a music supervisor and artist based in Rome. She holds a degree in Eastern Asian Languages and Cultures (Korean & Japanese), spent time living in Tokyo in 2013, and traces her musical instinct back to a childhood viewing of School of Rock.

After playing guitar and bass in a high-school punk band, she shifted to voice and piano, and in 2019 began writing as Kuni — a name borrowed from Tekken's Kunimitsu, a nod to a long fascination with Japan.

Her work as a music supervisor lives where these instincts converge: sourcing songs that hold a frame, clearing rights, and shaping the emotional grammar of a film's score.
